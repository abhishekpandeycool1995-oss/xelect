FAQ

SEO & Digital Marketing FAQ

Have questions about keyword strategies, page formatting, or off-page links? We have answers.

Google has said for years that high-quality, comprehensive content is the single most important ranking factor. Rather than packing a specific keyword density (which is an outdated tactic), structure your content naturally using LSI (Latent Semantic Indexing) keywords and address the user intent directly.
Premium page layouts place important content above the fold, use clean heading hierarchies (H1 to H4), structure paragraphs with bullet points for easy scanning, and maintain fast load speeds. Pages must also be completely responsive across all mobile and tablet viewports.
Off-page SEO refers to actions taken outside of your own website to impact rankings within search engine results pages. Creating backlinks from authoritative, niche-relevant websites signals credibility and trust to Google's ranking crawler algorithms.
High-quality content must be unique, detailed, and evidence-supported. Google uses search quality evaluator guidelines (E-E-A-T: Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness) to benchmark page indexing standards, making authority profiles and expert authorship crucial.
